How does psychosynthesis work?

A key assumption of psychosynthesis is that every human being has a vast potential for personal growth - a natural tendency to synthesize all aspects of their being to become the fullest realization of themselves. Psychosynthesis is a conscious attempt to cooperate with this natural process of personal development to foster awareness, self-healing, and a greater connection to the ever-changing nature of human life.

The therapeutic framework of psychosynthesis can help a wide range of individuals to overcome life challenges. Therapists in psychosynthesis counselling work to establish a specific relationship with their clients and will draw on a range of diverse techniques to guide them through the process of self-actualization and self-realisation.

About me

My name is Miloš [pronounced 'meelosh'], I am a Psychosynthesis Therapist in training (under supervision) at The Institute of Psychosynthesis London in affiliation with Middlesex University London.

My original career was as a Research Scientist in Physics and later on as a developer in a technological start-up. I increasingly found that I want to help people personally than trying to improve code or mathematical models so I started a psychotherapeutic path.

I found this passion while being involved in Leadership Training and Train the Trainer programs in the National Scout Organisation of Slovenia.

In my free time I love being in nature and observing its shapes and forms, I am reading at least 52 books a year and I am training my dog to become a rescue dog.

I am passionate about guiding, supporting, and helping people to understand who they are, where they are coming from, and where they want to go in the future.
